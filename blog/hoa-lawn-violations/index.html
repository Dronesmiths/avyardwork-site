HOA Lawn Violations? Here's How to Get Compliant Fast

By Brian, AV Yard Work  |  January 17, 2026  |  4 min read

Received an HOA violation notice about your lawn? Don't panic. Most violations can be resolved quickly with the right approach. Here's exactly what to do to get back in compliance and avoid fines in Palmdale, Lancaster, and the Antelope Valley.

Common HOA Lawn Violations in the Antelope Valley

1. Overgrown Grass or Weeds

Violation: Grass exceeds maximum height (typically 4-6 inches) or weeds are visible from the street.

Compliance deadline: Usually 7-14 days

Quick fix: Mow immediately, edge walkways, remove all visible weeds. For severely overgrown lawns, professional cleanup is fastest.

2. Dead or Brown Lawn

Violation: More than 25-50% of lawn is dead, brown, or bare dirt visible.

Compliance deadline: 14-30 days (longer because it requires reseeding/sodding)

Quick fix: Overseed bare spots immediately. For large dead areas, sod provides instant results. Increase watering to green up drought-stressed grass.

3. Unmaintained Landscaping

Violation: Overgrown shrubs, dead plants, debris accumulation, or unkempt appearance.

Compliance deadline: 7-14 days

Quick fix: Trim all shrubs and trees, remove dead plants, clear debris, edge all borders, blow hard surfaces clean.

4. Visible Weeds in Hardscape

Violation: Weeds growing through cracks in driveways, sidewalks, or decorative rock.

Compliance deadline: 7-10 days

Quick fix: Hand-pull or spray with herbicide. For cracks, use a weed torch or crack filler to prevent regrowth.

5. Irrigation Issues

Violation: Broken sprinklers watering sidewalks, water runoff into street, or visible leaks.

Compliance deadline: 3-7 days (water waste is urgent)

Quick fix: Repair broken heads immediately, adjust spray patterns, fix leaks. This often requires professional help.

Cost of Non-Compliance

HOA fines in the Antelope Valley typically follow this structure:

  • First notice: Warning only (7-14 day deadline)
  • Second notice: $25-$100 fine + 7 day deadline
  • Continued violation: $50-$200/week until resolved
  • HOA-hired cleanup: $500-$2,000 + lien on property
