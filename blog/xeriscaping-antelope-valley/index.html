Xeriscaping 101: Water-Efficient Landscaping for the Antelope Valley

By Brian, AV Yard Work  |  January 17, 2026  |  6 min read

With water costs rising and California's ongoing drought concerns, more Antelope Valley homeowners are discovering xeriscapingâ€”a landscaping approach that reduces water use by 50-75% while creating beautiful, low-maintenance outdoor spaces perfectly suited to our High Desert climate.

What is Xeriscaping?

Xeriscaping (pronounced "ZEER-ih-scaping") is water-efficient landscaping that uses drought-tolerant plants, efficient irrigation, and smart design principles. Despite myths, xeriscaping doesn't mean "zero-scaping" or ugly rock yardsâ€”it's about creating attractive landscapes that thrive naturally in arid climates.

Benefits of Xeriscaping in Palmdale & Lancaster

  • Massive water savings: Reduce outdoor water use by 50-75%
  • Lower water bills: Save $50-150+ per month in summer
  • Less maintenance: No weekly mowing, less fertilizing, minimal pruning
  • Climate-appropriate: Plants thrive naturally in High Desert conditions
  • Year-round color: Many desert plants bloom beautifully
  • Increased property value: Attractive, sustainable landscaping appeals to buyers

Best Plants for Antelope Valley Xeriscaping

Perennials & Groundcovers

  • Desert marigold: Bright yellow blooms, extremely drought-tolerant
  • California poppy: Orange flowers, native, self-seeding
  • Lavender: Purple flowers, fragrant, attracts pollinators
  • Rosemary: Evergreen, edible, low water needs
  • Yarrow: White/yellow flowers, spreads well

Shrubs

  • Texas ranger: Purple blooms after rain, very hardy
  • Desert willow: Pink flowers, attracts hummingbirds
  • Sage varieties: Silver foliage, aromatic
  • Creosote bush: Native, aromatic after rain

Succulents & Cacti

  • Agave: Architectural focal points, various sizes
  • Yucca: Tall flower spikes, dramatic
  • Barrel cactus: Low-maintenance, slow-growing
  • Prickly pear: Edible fruit, spreads moderately

7 Principles of Xeriscaping

1. Plan & Design

Group plants by water needs ("hydrozones") and consider sun exposure, slopes, and mature plant sizes.

2. Soil Improvement

Add compost to alkaline desert soil to improve drainage and water retention.

3. Reduce Turf Areas

Keep grass only in functional areas. Replace side yards and slopes with xeriscape.

4. Choose Drought-Tolerant Plants

Select plants native to arid climates that thrive with minimal water once established.

5. Efficient Irrigation

Use drip irrigation (90% efficient) instead of sprinklers (50-70% efficient).

6. Mulch Heavily

Apply 2-4 inches of decomposed granite or rock mulch to reduce evaporation by 50%+.

7. Maintain Appropriately

Weed regularly, prune annually, and adjust irrigation seasonally.

Common Xeriscaping Mistakes

  • Overwatering: #1 killer of desert plants. Water deeply but infrequently.
  • Using non-native plants: Tropical plants struggle in High Desert.
  • Skipping mulch: Bare soil loses moisture rapidly.
  • Planting too densely: Desert plants need space for roots.
