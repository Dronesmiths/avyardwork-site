Spring Lawn Prep Checklist for Palmdale & Lancaster Homeowners

By Brian, AV Yard Work  |  January 17, 2026  |  5 min read

Spring is the most critical time for lawn care in the Antelope Valley. The work you do in March and April determines whether your lawn thrives or struggles through the brutal summer heat. This checklist covers everything you need to do to prepare your Palmdale or Lancaster lawn for the growing season ahead.

Why Spring Prep Matters in the High Desert

The Antelope Valley's extreme climate makes spring preparation especially important:

  • Short growing window: Grass has only 6-8 weeks to strengthen before 100¬∞F+ temperatures arrive
  • Weed explosion: Desert weeds germinate aggressively in March-April if not controlled
  • Compacted soil: Winter rain and foot traffic compact alkaline soil, restricting root growth
  • Irrigation issues: Systems often develop problems over winter that waste water in summer

üí° Best Time to Act: Mid-March through mid-April is the optimal window. Too early and you risk frost damage; too late and weeds have already germinated.

March Tasks: Foundation Work

1. Remove Winter Debris

Clear all fallen leaves, branches, and wind-blown trash from your lawn. Debris blocks sunlight and traps moisture, promoting disease.

  • Rake thoroughly to remove dead grass (thatch)
  • Clear gutters and downspouts
  • Remove any accumulated tumbleweeds

2. Aerate Compacted Soil

This is THE most important spring task for High Desert lawns. Alkaline soil compacts easily, preventing water and nutrients from reaching roots.

  • Use a core aerator (pulls plugs of soil out)
  • Aerate when soil is slightly moist, not bone dry or soggy
  • Focus on high-traffic areas and clay-heavy zones
  • Leave soil plugs on lawn‚Äîthey'll break down naturally

3. Apply Pre-Emergent Weed Control

Pre-emergent herbicide prevents weed seeds from germinating. Apply in early March before soil temperatures reach 55¬∞F.

  • Target crabgrass, spurge, and other annual weeds
  • Water lightly after application to activate
  • Don't seed for 6-8 weeks after applying pre-emergent

4. Prune Trees and Shrubs

Late winter/early spring is the best time to prune dormant plants before new growth starts.

  • Remove dead, diseased, or crossing branches
  • Thin canopies to allow sunlight to reach lawn
  • Shape hedges and ornamental plants

5. Repair Bare Spots

Fix thin or bare areas before summer heat makes establishment difficult.

  • Loosen soil in bare spots
  • Add compost or topsoil if needed
  • Seed or lay sod (match existing grass type)
  • Keep newly seeded areas consistently moist

April Tasks: Growth & Protection

6. Fertilize Warm-Season Grasses

As Bermuda and Buffalo grass green up, give them a nitrogen boost to promote thick growth.

  • Wait until grass is 50% green before fertilizing
  • Use slow-release fertilizer (16-4-8 or similar)
  • Apply when rain is forecast or water in thoroughly
  • Avoid over-fertilizing‚Äîit promotes disease and thatch

7. Increase Watering Gradually

As temperatures rise, your lawn needs more water. Transition slowly from winter to summer schedules.

  • Early April: Water once per week
  • Mid-April: Water twice per week
  • Late April/May: Water 2-3 times per week
  • Always water in early morning (4-8 AM)

8. Resume Weekly Mowing

Once grass starts actively growing, return to a regular mowing schedule.

  • Start with a slightly lower height to remove winter growth
  • Gradually raise to summer height (see grass-specific recommendations)
  • Sharpen mower blades for clean cuts
  • Mulch clippings for free nitrogen

9. Inspect & Tune Irrigation System

Run your system during daylight to check for problems before summer heat arrives.

  • Look for broken or clogged sprinkler heads
  • Adjust heads to eliminate overspray onto sidewalks
  • Check for leaks in pipes and valves
  • Test controller and replace battery if needed
  • Flush drip lines to remove mineral buildup

10. Mulch Garden Beds

Add 2-3 inches of mulch around trees, shrubs, and flower beds to retain moisture.

  • Use decomposed granite or bark mulch
  • Keep mulch 2-3 inches away from plant stems
  • Mulch reduces water evaporation by 50%+

Spring Prep Mistakes to Avoid

‚ùå Fertilizing Too Early

Fertilizing dormant grass wastes money and promotes weed growth. Wait until grass is actively growing and at least 50% green.

‚ùå Scalping the Lawn

Cutting grass too short in spring stresses plants and allows weeds to establish. Follow the 1/3 rule‚Äînever remove more than one-third of blade height.

‚ùå Overwatering

Cool spring temperatures mean grass needs less water than summer. Overwatering promotes fungal disease and shallow roots.

‚ùå Skipping Aeration

This is the #1 mistake in the High Desert. Compacted alkaline soil MUST be aerated annually for healthy root development.
