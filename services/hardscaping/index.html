Hardscaping FAQs

Common questions about hardscape design, paver installation, and outdoor living spaces in the Antelope Valley.

What materials work best for High Desert hardscaping?

Concrete pavers, natural stone, and decorative rock are all excellent choices for the Antelope Valley. We recommend UV-resistant pavers with proper sealing to prevent fading and materials that can withstand temperature extremes.

How long does a typical hardscaping project take?

Small projects like borders or walkways typically take 2-3 days. Medium-sized patios require 3-5 days, while large custom projects may take 1-2 weeks depending on complexity and weather.

Do pavers crack in the High Desert climate?

Quality pavers installed with proper base preparation rarely crack. We use techniques specifically designed for desert conditions, including proper drainage, compacted base, and expansion joints where needed.

Can you match existing hardscaping or extend current features?

Yes. We can match existing paver patterns, colors, and styles to seamlessly extend patios, walkways, or other hardscape features. We'll help source matching materials or suggest complementary alternatives.

Do you offer design services or just installation?

We offer complete design consultation at no additional charge. We'll work with you to create a custom plan that fits your vision, budget, and propertyâ€”then provide professional installation.

How do I maintain my new hardscaping?

Most hardscaping requires minimal maintenanceâ€”occasional sweeping, rinsing, and resealing every few years. We provide detailed care instructions and can perform periodic maintenance if needed.
