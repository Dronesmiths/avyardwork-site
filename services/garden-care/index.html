Desert Garden Care FAQs

Common questions about xeriscape maintenance and native plant care in the Antelope Valley.

What is xeriscape gardening?

Xeriscaping is water-efficient landscaping using drought-tolerant plants native to arid climates. In the Antelope Valley, this includes succulents, agave, desert willow, and other species that thrive with minimal water.

How often do desert gardens need maintenance?

Most established xeriscapes need professional maintenance monthly or bi-weekly, depending on plant types and season. We adjust schedules based on growth patterns and seasonal needs.

Can you help convert my traditional lawn to xeriscape?

Absolutely. We specialize in desert garden design and installation, helping homeowners transition from water-intensive lawns to beautiful, sustainable xeriscapes that reduce water bills dramatically.

What native plants work best in the High Desert?

Popular choices include California poppies, desert marigold, brittlebush, Joshua trees, and various agave and yucca species. We recommend plants based on your specific microclimate and aesthetic preferences.

How do you protect desert gardens from extreme heat?

We adjust watering schedules, apply protective mulch, provide temporary shade for sensitive plants, and ensure irrigation systems are functioning optimally during summer heat waves.

Do you offer organic garden care options?

Yes. We prioritize natural pest control methods and organic fertilizers that work well with native plants and don't harm beneficial desert insects and pollinators.
